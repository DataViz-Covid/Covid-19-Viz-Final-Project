What is the website designed for?

Welcome to our COVID-19 data analysis website! Our site features 12 comprehensive graphs that provide insight into the impact of the pandemic on different societies. From infection rates to vaccination progress and economic trends, we've got you covered. Our aim is to provide a centralized platform where you can easily access up-to-date and accurate information about the COVID-19 pandemic. We have carefully curated the data to ensure that our users can get a clear picture of the pandemic's impact on various aspects of society. Our user-friendly interface allows you to explore the data and customize your viewing experience to suit your needs. You can filter the data by location, time period, and other relevant parameters to gain deeper insights into how the pandemic has affected different regions. Whether you're a researcher, policymaker, or simply interested in understanding the impact of the pandemic on society, our website is the perfect resource for you. So, come and explore our graphs and gain a deeper understanding of how COVID-19 has affected our world.



Part 1- Covid Progress


1- Daily Covid Cases

Line chart that shows the daily number of new covid cases worldwide, by continents or even by countries




2- Number of Covid Cases with respect to Population Density

This graph is a scatter plot that represents the total number of cases of covid for each country based on its population density. The countries are color coded based on their continents, and hovering over the legend will show the data of this particular continent alone. The scales can be changed from log to linear.




3- Timeseries of Number of Covid Infections and Hospitalizations

Here based on time, we show the total number of people infected by covid and the total number of people hospitalized on the same date. It begins with zero cases prior to covid and gradually increases. As shown by the line graphs, the hospitality rate has one peak that corresponds to the second peak of the cases line. As the pointer moves along the lines, more information on accurate numbers for each of these measures will be displayed. Because the ranges of the two graphs differ, there are two y axes provided for each.




4- Highest Weekly Hospitalization Rate vs Population Data

the goal of this scatter plot is to investigate if there is a correlation between the highest weekly rate of hospitalization per million and different population facts such as the the percentage of elderly people (65 and older, or 70 and older), the median age and the life expectancy. Here the data is mostly focus in Europe with a few datapoints elsewhere.




5- Map of Covid Measures in Time

This map shows how covid restrictions started being implemented in time, and which countries had the strictest restrictions




Part 2- Vaccination Phase


6- Daily Number of Vaccine Doses

This is a line graph that represents the total number of vaccine doses by day, either for each country or for each continent. Selecting the continent option will allow the user to further choose which continents to display the data of, and clicking on the name of the continent on the legend will show the data of the countries of this continent only. Click "back" to return to the previous graph. Instead choosing the countries option allows the user to either show the data of some of the countries with a high number of vaccines, or to show the full data of all countries.




7- Vaccine Doses based on Population Demographics

In this series of bar charts, we will compare the percentage of the population in each country who receives each dose of covid vaccines (first, second, and third dose). You can also change the age range using the dropdown menu to see which age group in which country receives the most vaccine shots.




8- Vaccine Manufacturers Distribution

Another graph is based on covid-19 vaccines, but this time we'll compare vaccine manufacturers. Choose a country from the dropdown menu, and then compare the vaccine usage in each of these countries. You can point to each part to see the percentage of the whole for each vaccine manufacturer in order to get the exact percentage of each fraction.




Part 3- Aftermath


9- Flow Graph

In this graph, we attempt to display distribution of population and how their contamination state is and the consequence of the contamination. The data is of the 31st of December 2022.




10- Map of Covid Deaths per Million

This is a map of the number of deaths due to covid per million. Lighter colors mean a lower amount per million and as the color darkens it means a higher amount of deaths per million.




11- Cartogram of GDP before vs after Covid

This is a cartogram of the GDP of different countries before (December 2019) and after covid (December 2021). The slider allows to compare the difference between different regions on a macro scale and see how different region's economy fare after COVID.




12- Change in Inflation after Covid

This graph is a bar chart with both positive and negative values that represents the change in inflation for each country. This graph investigates the potential impact of covid on the economies of various countries, indicating which countries' inflation increased and which countries' inflation decreased following covid-19.
